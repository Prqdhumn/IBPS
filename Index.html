Final Merit Calculation

The final merit list is prepared based on the marks obtained in the **Main Examination and the Interview**. The Preliminary exam is only for qualification and its marks are not considered for the final score.

The weightage given to the Mains and Interview is in the ratio of **80:20** respectively. This highlights the immense importance of scoring well in the Mains exam, while a good interview performance can be a significant deciding factor.

Prelims Exam Pattern (Qualifying)

The Prelims is an online exam with 100 questions for 100 marks, to be completed in 60 minutes. Each section has a strict time limit of 20 minutes. There is a **negative marking of 0.25 marks** for each wrong answer. You must clear both sectional and overall cut-offs to qualify for the Mains.

  • Total Questions: 100
  • Total Marks: 100
  • Total Duration: 60 Minutes (Sectional Timing)

Mains Exam Pattern (Merit-Based)

The Mains exam is the most critical stage. It consists of an objective test and a descriptive test, both held online on the same day. There is a **negative marking of 0.25 marks** for each wrong answer in the objective part.

Objective Test:

  • Total Questions: 145
  • Total Marks: 200
  • Total Duration: 160 Minutes

Descriptive Test:

  • Tasks: 1 Essay & 1 Letter
  • Total Marks: 25
  • Total Duration: 30 Minutes

Ace The Interview

The final hurdle. Prepare to showcase your personality, knowledge, and suitability for a banking career.

Key Focus Areas

  • Banking Knowledge: Understand basic terms, financial concepts, and the role of the RBI.
  • Current Affairs: Be aware of recent national, international, and economic events.
  • Personal Profile: Be ready to discuss your background, strengths, weaknesses, and why you want a banking career.

Interview Structure

  • Duration: 15-20 minutes
  • Marks: 100
  • Weightage: 20% in final merit list
  • Minimum Score: 40% to qualify

Top Tips for Success

  • ✓Project confidence and maintain a professional demeanor.
  • ✓Communicate clearly and be honest in your answers.
  • ✓Do not argue with the interview panel; maintain a respectful tone.
  • ✓Practice mock interviews to build communication skills and reduce anxiety.
